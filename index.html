Bio

 As a Ph.D. student at IMEC and Ghent University, I have a deep passion for the field of image processing and imaging. My research interests primarily focus on tensor completion, hyperspectral imaging, and low-level vision. In particular, I specialize in hyperspectral demosaicing, denoising, high-dimensional tensor completion, and RGB image restoration. In my work, I enjoy applying mathematics to develop novel methods for tackling complex image processing problems. I have published several papers on these topics in journals such as IEEE Transactions on Multimedia, IEEE Transactions on Geoscience and Remote Sensing, Computer Vision and Image Understanding, Signal Processing, and IEEE Transactions on Computational Imaging, among others. I am committed to advancing the field of image processing through my research, and I look forward to continuing my work in this exciting and challenging field.

 I work in the field of remote sensing information processing, computer vision, and machine learning. Currently, I specifically focus on the following research topics:

  • Hyperspectral imaging (HI) : MSFA imaging, Hyperspectral camera processing pipeline
  • Tensor Completion: Deep Learning, Low-Rank Tensor Representation, Model-Data-Driven
